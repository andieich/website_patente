In April 2025, I completed a three-week sampling campaign across three reef sites on Mo'orea — Temae, Tiahura, and Pihaena — building on baseline data collected in 2023. The campaign focused on **coral host–microbiome dynamics** following the unprecedented thermal stress event of February 2024.

## Sites & methods

At each site, we tagged 30 colonies of *Pocillopora meandrina*, *Acropora hyacinthus*, and *Porites lobata* and collected paired tissue + mucus samples for 16S rRNA sequencing. Reef-scale benthic surveys were conducted using photo-quadrats along permanent transects, analysed in CoralNet.

## Preliminary findings

Visual bleaching had largely receded by sampling time, but **community composition signals tell a different story**:

- *Pocillopora* colonies showed elevated *Vibrio* and reduced *Endozoicomonas* abundance compared to baseline.
- *Porites* maintained more stable communities — consistent with their reputation as thermal generalists.
- Sediment microbial communities at Pihaena (closest to the river mouth) differed strongly from the other two sites.

A more complete analysis is in preparation for the *Coral Reefs* journal. The full sequencing pipeline is reproducible from the [project repository](https://github.com/andieich) — open data, open code.
